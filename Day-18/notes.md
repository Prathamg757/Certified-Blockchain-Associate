# 3. Token Economics

## Token

A Token is a digital unit of value stored on a blockchain.

---

## Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of converting a real-world asset into digital tokens.

### Examples

```
Gold → Gold Token

House → House Token
```

---

## Features of Tokens

- Digital Representation of Assets
- Transferable
- Programmable
- Immutable Ownership

---

# Types of Tokens

## 1. Equity Tokens

Represent ownership in a company.

---

## 2. Utility Tokens

Provide access to services on a platform.

---

## 3. Security Tokens

Represent regulated financial assets.

---

## 4. Asset Tokens

Represent real-world assets.

Examples:

- Gold
- Real Estate

---

## 5. Currency Tokens

Used as digital money.

Examples:

- Bitcoin
- Stablecoins

---

# 4. NFT (Non-Fungible Token)

## Definition

An NFT is a unique digital asset stored on a blockchain.

---

# Fungible vs Non-Fungible

## Fungible Assets

Can be exchanged equally.

Examples:

- ₹100 = ₹100
- 1 BTC = 1 BTC

---

## Non-Fungible Assets

Unique and cannot be exchanged equally.

Examples:

- Mona Lisa Painting
- House
- Birth Certificate
- NFT Artwork

---

# Ethereum Token Standards

| Standard | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|
| ERC-20 | Fungible Tokens |
| ERC-721 | NFTs |

---

# NFT Use Cases

- Digital Art
- Gaming Assets
- Creator Economy
- DeFi Collateral

---

# Benefits of NFTs

- Scarcity
- Uniqueness
- Ownership Proof
- Security

---
